Fine-grained Emotion Controllable Text-to-speech using Natural Language Prompts via VAD-aligned Prompt-Speech Matching

 

Overall framework of Venom


Abstract

 

Text-to-speech (TTS) technologies have substantially evolved, integrating natural language prompts to provide user-friendly control over speech styles and emotions. However, existing prompt-based TTS systems often rely on basic categorical emotional labels, limiting the range and depth of expressiveness in synthesized speech. This study introduces Venom, a novel TTS system that leverages valence-arousal-dominance (VAD) values for fine-grained emotional control through natural language prompts. By aligning VAD-based values with maximum likelihood estimation for prompt-speech matching, Venom ensures that the synthesized speech accurately captures the intended emotional nuances and speaker characteristics. Experimental results reveal that Venom produces emotionally diverse and expressive speech, setting a new standard for emotion-specific speech synthesis. This approach highlights the potential of detailed emotional control to enhance the expressiveness and naturalness of synthesized speech, marking considerable progress in TTS technology.
